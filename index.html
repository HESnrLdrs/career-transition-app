A Rather Warm Welcome from Prof Thomson

"Dear colleague, I'm absolutely delighted to be your guide through this important journey. Having spent over two decades navigating the increasingly complex landscape of UK higher education - from the 2008 financial crisis through Brexit uncertainty, COVID disruption, and now the current funding pressures - I've learnt that transition isn't just about changing jobs. It's about reimagining your professional identity whilst maintaining the essence of who you are."
Initial Reflection

Before we begin, take a moment to reflect on what brought you to this workbook:

What are your main reasons for considering a career transition at this time?

Module 1

Ready for a Fresh Start

Where Are You Now?
"Before we can chart a course forward, we must understand precisely where we are. Not where we think we should be, or where others expect us to be, but where we actually are right now."
Exercise 1.1: Your Identity Architecture Assessment

For each layer, rate your current satisfaction (1-10) and write a brief reflection:

🏛️ Professional Identity (Current Role & Status)

Rate your satisfaction with your current professional position:

Reflection prompts:

  • How do you feel about your current role and responsibilities?
  • What aspects of your professional identity energise you?
  • What feels misaligned or draining?
Module 2

Building Confidence

Reconnecting with Your Strengths
"Here's something rather wonderful I've discovered: the very skills that make you brilliant as an academic are precisely what will make you brilliant in whatever comes next."

📊 Research Insight

Studies show that academics who successfully transition to new careers typically leverage at least 70% of their existing skills in their new roles.

Exercise 2.1: Academic Skills Translation

Identify your core academic skills and how they translate to other sectors:

🧠 Analytical Mastery

How this skill applies outside academia:

Data analysis, problem-solving, strategic thinking, research capabilities

💬 Communication Excellence

How this skill applies outside academia:

Presentation skills, stakeholder communication, training and development

Module 3

Planning What's Next

Your Purpose Journey
"The question isn't 'What job should I get next?' The question is 'How can I best use my talents, experience, and passion to create meaningful impact?'"

💡 Prof Thomson's Tip

Start with purpose rather than position. When you begin with what truly matters to you, the path becomes remarkably clear.

Exercise 3.1: Your Ikigai Exploration

Discover the intersection of what you love, what you're good at, what the world needs, and what you can be paid for:

💝 What You Love

What activities make you lose track of time?

What topics could you discuss for hours?

🌍 What the World Needs

What problems in the world concern you most?

What issues are you uniquely positioned to address?

Module 4

Making It Happen

Implementation & Support
"Right then, enough reflecting - time for action! But not the frantic, scatter-gun approach that many academics take when they panic about their future. We're going to be strategic, systematic, and sensible about this transition."
Exercise 4.1: Your 90-Day Action Plan

Create a realistic plan for your transition over the next three months:

📅 Month 1: Exploration

Goals: Complete 5 informational interviews, update LinkedIn profile, identify target sectors

📅 Month 2: Skill Development

Goals: Address skill gaps, attend relevant workshops, begin networking in target industries

📅 Month 3: Implementation

Goals: Apply for positions, develop portfolio, secure references
